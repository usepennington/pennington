A fragrant, creamy curry that comes together in under 30 minutes. Use store-bought paste for speed or make your own for the best flavour.

## Ingredients

- 2 tbsp green curry paste
- 400ml coconut milk
- 200ml chicken or vegetable stock
- 1 tbsp fish sauce
- 1 tbsp palm sugar (or brown sugar)
- 500g chicken thigh, sliced (or tofu for vegetarian)
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 150g green beans, trimmed and halved
- 1 small aubergine, cubed
- Handful of Thai basil leaves
- 2 kaffir lime leaves
- 1 tbsp vegetable oil
- Jasmine rice, to serve

## Instructions

1. Heat the oil in a wok or large pan over medium-high heat. Fry the curry paste for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
2. Add the thick cream from the top of the coconut milk and stir to combine. Cook for 2 minutes.
3. Add the chicken (or tofu) and cook until lightly sealed — about 3 minutes.
4. Pour in the remaining coconut milk and stock. Add fish sauce, sugar, and lime leaves.
5. Bring to a gentle simmer and add the aubergine. Cook for 10 minutes.
6. Add the bell pepper and green beans. Cook for another 5 minutes until tender.
7. Remove from heat, stir in the Thai basil, and serve over jasmine rice.

## Tips

- Tear the kaffir lime leaves slightly before adding to release more flavour.
- For a richer curry, use only coconut milk (no stock) and add a second can.
- Thai basil wilts quickly — add it right at the end, off the heat.
