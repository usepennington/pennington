A rich, moist chocolate cake that comes together easily. The secret is using hot coffee to bloom the cocoa powder and create an incredibly deep chocolate flavour.

## Ingredients

### Cake

- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 2 cups sugar
- 3/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
- 2 tsp baking soda
- 1 tsp salt
- 2 eggs
- 1 cup buttermilk
- 1 cup hot coffee
- 1/2 cup vegetable oil
- 2 tsp vanilla extract

### Ganache Frosting

- 350g dark chocolate, chopped
- 300ml heavy cream
- 2 tbsp butter

## Instructions

1. Preheat the oven to 175°C (350°F). Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.
2. Whisk together the flour, sugar, cocoa, baking soda, and salt in a large bowl.
3. Add the eggs, buttermilk, oil, and vanilla. Beat until combined.
4. Stir in the hot coffee — the batter will be very thin, and that's correct.
5. Divide between the prepared pans and bake for 30-35 minutes until a toothpick comes out clean.
6. Cool in pans for 10 minutes, then turn out onto wire racks to cool completely.

### For the ganache

1. Heat the cream until it just begins to simmer.
2. Pour over the chopped chocolate and let sit for 2 minutes.
3. Stir from the centre outward until smooth. Add butter and stir until melted.
4. Let cool until spreadable — about 1 hour at room temperature.
5. Spread between layers and over the top and sides of the cake.

## Tips

- The coffee enhances the chocolate flavour without making the cake taste like coffee.
- For the best texture, bring all refrigerated ingredients to room temperature before starting.
