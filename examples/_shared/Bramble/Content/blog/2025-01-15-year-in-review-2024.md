When I shipped the first commit to what would become Bramble in a rented apartment in early 2022, I told myself it was a personal experiment. I was going to see if a language designed around `Result` and `Option` from day one — no null, no surprise panics, ownership-lite — could still feel lightweight enough to script with. Two years later, 2024 was the year that experiment became a real project, with real users, real bugs, and real momentum.

## The year in releases

| Version | Date | Headline |
|---------|------|----------|
| 1.0 | Jan 2024 | Stable language spec, standard library, Thicket package registry |
| Trellis | Feb 2024 | Declarative build system, `Trellisfile`, incremental compilation |
| 1.1 | Jun 2024 | Pattern-matching improvements, better error messages, `sprig fmt --check` |
| Hedge | Sep 2024 | Browser playground, Wasm-compiled patch, shareable snippet URLs |
| 1.2 | Dec 2024 | Async tasks, cooperative scheduler, typed channels |

That's five meaningful milestones in twelve months, none of which I planned in January with any confidence we'd actually ship them.

## What surprised me

Trellis surprised me the most. I expected the build system to be a quiet infrastructure release — something tooling-oriented developers would appreciate and everyone else would ignore. Instead it turned out to be the thing that brought in teams building larger projects. The [build graph explanation](xref:bramble.explanation.the-build-graph) has become one of the most-read pages on the docs site, which tells me people are actively trying to understand how incremental builds work, not just using them as a black box.

The Hedge playground surprised me in the opposite direction. We built it expecting power users to use it for demos. What actually happened: educators and people evaluating the language started sending Hedge links to each other before any of us noticed. Over a third of new installs in Q4 came from users who said they first ran Bramble in Hedge.

## Growth, honestly reported

The package registry at `thicket.dev` ended 2024 with 412 published packages, up from 0 at the start of the year. Weekly active installs grew steadily through the year, with a visible spike after the 1.2 announcement. Fernweb and Tinroof both started using Bramble in production this year. The Discord crossed 2,000 members in November.

I'm deliberately not publishing raw download numbers. They're easy to game and harder to interpret than they look. The metrics I care about are "do people come back?" and "do they bring others?" Both trends look good.

## Thank you

This project has an unfair amount of talented people for its size. Maple carried the language design and the async work. Rowan built Hedge and most of the developer experience layer. Theo found and fixed more bugs than anyone should have to. Juniper, Hazel, and Indira came on this year and immediately made everything better. None of this happens without them.

If you're reading this and you filed a bug, wrote a package, made a tutorial, or just told a colleague "you should try Bramble" — that counts. More than you'd think.

More in 2025. We're just getting started.
