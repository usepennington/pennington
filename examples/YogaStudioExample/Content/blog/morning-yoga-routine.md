I used to think a yoga practice had to be a 90-minute affair. Candles lit, playlist curated, the whole production. Then I had a kid.

These days my morning practice is 15 minutes — sometimes squeezed between making oatmeal and finding lost shoes. And honestly? It's the most transformative practice I've ever had.

## Why 15 Minutes Works

The research is clear: consistency matters more than duration. A daily 15-minute practice creates more lasting change than a weekly 90-minute class. Your nervous system responds to regularity.

## The Sequence

Here's my exact morning flow. Time it if you want — it clocks in at about 14 minutes with breathing.

### Minutes 1-3: Breathe & Ground

Start seated. Don't overthink the posture — a chair works fine.

- Close your eyes
- Take 10 slow breaths, counting each exhale
- Set an intention for the day (one word works)

### Minutes 3-6: Wake Up the Spine

```
Cat-Cow × 8 rounds
    ↓
Downward Dog (hold 5 breaths)
    ↓
Ragdoll Forward Fold (hold 5 breaths)
    ↓
Halfway Lift → Forward Fold × 3
```

### Minutes 6-10: Sun Salutation A

One full round of Surya Namaskar A:

```
Mountain Pose
    ↓ inhale, arms up
Standing Backbend
    ↓ exhale, fold
Forward Fold
    ↓ inhale, halfway lift
Halfway Lift
    ↓ exhale, step/jump back
Chaturanga
    ↓ inhale
Upward Dog
    ↓ exhale
Downward Dog (5 breaths)
    ↓ step/jump forward
Forward Fold
    ↓ inhale, rise
Mountain Pose
```

Repeat 2-3 times depending on your pace.

### Minutes 10-13: Choose Your Focus

This is where you make the sequence yours. Pick ONE area:

**Hips tight?** Pigeon pose, 90 seconds each side.

**Shoulders tense?** Thread the needle, 60 seconds each side, then eagle arms.

**Back aching?** Supine twist, 90 seconds each side.

### Minutes 13-15: Savasana

Yes, even in 15 minutes, take savasana. Even 2 minutes of lying still integrates everything you just did. This isn't optional — it's where the magic happens.

## Making It Stick

Three tips from someone who's maintained a daily practice for 16 years:

1. **Same time, same place.** Your body will start craving it.
2. **Lower the bar.** On bad days, just do the breathing. It still counts.
3. **Never negotiate with your morning self.** Decide the night before.
