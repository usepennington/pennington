If you're reading this, you're probably thinking about trying yoga but feeling some combination of curious and terrified. Good news: that's exactly how I felt before my first class.

Spoiler: it was fine. Better than fine, actually.

## What Yoga Actually Is

Let's clear something up: yoga isn't about touching your toes. It's about what happens on the way down.

At its core, yoga is a practice of connecting breath to movement. Everything else — the flexibility, the strength, the Instagram poses — is a side effect.

## Choosing Your First Class

Not all yoga is created equal. Here's a quick guide:

> [!TIP]
> At 937 Yoga, our Saturday **Community Flow** is specifically designed for beginners. It's the perfect first class.

### Best for Beginners

**Vinyasa Flow (All Levels)** — Flowing sequences linked to breath. "All Levels" means the instructor will offer modifications. This is the most popular style for a reason.

**Yin Yoga** — Slow, passive stretching. Poses held for 3-5 minutes. Zero athletic ability required. Great for people who think they "can't do yoga."

**Restorative** — Like a nap, but better. You lie on props and let gravity do everything. Literally anyone can do this.

### Wait Until Later

**Ashtanga** — A set sequence that builds over months. Wonderful, but intense. Give it a few months.

**Hot Yoga** — Adding 95°F heat to a new practice is a lot. Get comfortable with the poses first.

**Power Yoga** — Essentially a strength workout in yoga form. Great once you know the basics.

## What to Expect in Your First Class

### Before Class
- Arrive 10-15 minutes early
- Wear comfortable clothes (no jeans, no zippers)
- Bring water; we provide everything else
- Tell the instructor it's your first class (they'll keep an eye on you)

### During Class
- You will lose track of your left and right. Everyone does.
- You will look at your neighbor for guidance. That's fine.
- You might feel emotional. That's normal and more common than you'd think.
- If something hurts, stop. Discomfort is okay; pain is not.

### After Class
- You'll probably feel amazing
- You might be surprisingly sore the next day
- You'll wonder why you didn't start sooner

## Common Myths, Busted

> [!WARNING]
> Don't let these myths keep you from the mat.

**"I'm not flexible enough for yoga."**
That's like saying you're too dirty to take a shower. Yoga *creates* flexibility.

**"Yoga is just stretching."**
Come to one of James's Power Yoga classes and get back to me.

**"I need special equipment."**
Comfortable clothes and showing up. That's it. We provide mats and props.

**"Yoga is only for women."**
Yoga was invented by men and practiced exclusively by men for thousands of years. Everyone belongs on the mat.

**"I'm too old/heavy/injured."**
Yoga is infinitely modifiable. If you can breathe, you can do yoga.

## Your First Month Plan

Here's what I tell every beginner:

**Week 1:** Come to one class. Any class marked "All Levels."

**Week 2:** Come twice. Try a different style.

**Week 3:** Start noticing what you like. Yin? Vinyasa? Meditation?

**Week 4:** Establish your rhythm. Two to three classes per week is the sweet spot.
