I used to think yoga had to be this whole 90-minute production. Candles, vibes, the aesthetic. Then life happened and now my practice is 15 minutes between making breakfast and finding my keys.

Plot twist: it's the best my practice has ever been.

## Why 15 Minutes Hits Different

Science says consistency beats duration every time. A daily 15-minute practice does more for your nervous system than a weekly 90-minute class. Your body literally craves routine.

## The Sequence (no gatekeeping)

This is my exact morning flow and it's genuinely unmatched.

### Minutes 1-3: Reset Your Brain

Sit anywhere. A chair is valid. Close your eyes, take 10 slow breaths, and set an intention. One word. That's it. Low effort, high impact.

### Minutes 3-6: Wake Up the Spine

Cat-Cow into Downward Dog into Forward Fold. Your spine will literally thank you.

### Minutes 6-10: Sun Salutation

The classic Surya Namaskar A. It's a classic for a reason — the sequence is *chef's kiss*.

### Minutes 10-13: Customize (pick your fighter)

**Hips locked up?** Pigeon pose, 90 seconds each side.

**Shoulders living in your ears?** Thread the needle, then eagle arms.

**Back being dramatic?** Supine twist both sides.

### Minutes 13-15: Savasana (non-negotiable)

Yes, even in 15 minutes, you LIE DOWN. This is where the benefits actually integrate. Skipping savasana is like leaving the movie before the post-credits scene.

## Making It Stick

Three tips that go hard:

1. **Same time, same spot.** Your body will start expecting it.
2. **Lower the bar.** Bad day? Just breathe. Still counts.
3. **Decide the night before.** Don't negotiate with morning-you, they can't be trusted.
