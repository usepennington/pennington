Breathing is the one bodily function we do both consciously and unconsciously. That dual nature makes it a powerful bridge between mind and body — and the key to managing stress in real time.

Here are five techniques I teach at 937 Yoga, adapted from classical pranayama for modern practitioners.

## 1. Box Breathing (Sama Vritti)

Equal-part breathing is the Swiss Army knife of breathwork. Navy SEALs use it before missions. You can use it before meetings.

> [!TIP]
> Start with 4-count cycles. As you get comfortable, work up to 6 or 8 counts.

**The pattern:**
- Inhale for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts
- Exhale for 4 counts
- Hold for 4 counts

Repeat for 4-8 rounds.

## 2. Extended Exhale

When your exhale is longer than your inhale, you activate the parasympathetic nervous system — your body's built-in calm-down switch.

**The pattern:**
- Inhale for 4 counts
- Exhale for 6-8 counts

This is my go-to recommendation for anyone dealing with anxiety.

## 3. Alternate Nostril Breathing (Nadi Shodhana)

This technique balances the left and right hemispheres of the brain. It sounds esoteric, but the research backs it up — studies show it reduces blood pressure and improves focus.

> [!NOTE]
> If one nostril is congested, don't force it. Simply visualize the breath flowing through both sides equally.

**The pattern:**
1. Close right nostril with thumb, inhale through left
2. Close left nostril with ring finger, exhale through right
3. Inhale through right
4. Close right, exhale through left
5. That's one round — do 5-10

## 4. Lion's Breath (Simhasana)

This one looks ridiculous and that's part of the point. Lion's breath releases tension in the jaw, face, and throat — where most of us hold stress without realizing it.

**The technique:**
- Inhale deeply through the nose
- Open mouth wide, stick tongue out, exhale forcefully with a "HA" sound
- Bonus: widen your eyes

> [!IMPORTANT]
> Don't do this in a quiet office. Save it for the car, the shower, or the mat.

## 5. 4-7-8 Breathing

Popularized by Dr. Andrew Weil, this technique is essentially a natural tranquilizer. It's particularly effective for falling asleep.

**The pattern:**
- Inhale through nose for 4 counts
- Hold for 7 counts
- Exhale through mouth for 8 counts

Two rounds of this and most people feel noticeably calmer.

## Finding Your Practice

The best breathing technique is the one you'll actually do. Start with box breathing — it's simple, discreet, and effective. Once that feels natural, experiment with the others.
